RYTEN LAB is a research team in the department of Molecular Neuroscience within UCL Institute of Neurology (ION). The lab is located at Russell Square House. Ryten Lab is a interdisciplinary group brining together practical , experimental and methodological researchers in the areas of Medicine , Computer Science and Biology. The group has collaborations with clinicians and NHS hospital. The overall objective of the team is to develop numerical algorithms and methods of neurological brain diseases from patient data (clinical and genomic data). In order to achieve this objectives, we develop new models of data representation and applying statistical approaches that can intergrate different dataset samples acquired from patient post- morten human tissues. These data-set in form of tissues and genes are then expressed using different statistical and methodological approaches such as ; gene expression, weighted co-expression network analysis (WGCNA). In turn these models will allow for adequate or to some extend help in the identification of gene expression. This process will allow us to acquire better understaing of the nature of the genes co-expressed and provide avenue to understand the disease progression which in turn will lead to new decision to support the diagnosis and design of clinical trials. Models developed in the group are being created as software packages that could be use by researchers for further analysis.

Methodological concentrations:
  • Machine Learning
  • WGCNA
  • K-Means
  • Statistical processess (e.g gene correlation with the eigengene)
  • Graphical analysis of outcomes
  • eQTL analysis

Disease applications:
  • Parkinson’s diseases
  • Alzheimer’s disease
  • Demetia’s disease
