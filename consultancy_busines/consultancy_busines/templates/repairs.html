DANIEL ONAH, Ph.D. Biography Dr Onah, a native born of Nigeria is an academic of strong international standing in Computer Science and Computer Systems Engineering (Software Systems). Dr Onah received his first degree in BSc. Computer Science from University of Uyo in Nigeria and received the MSc (Eng.) degree in Computer Systems Engineering with specialisation in Software Systems from the University of East London, United Kingdom in 2011. He is currently a Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science, University of Warwick, United Kingdom. Dr Onah is a member of the Intelligent & Adaptive Systems Research Group in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Warwick. His research focuses on educational technology and the design of e-learning systems and adaptive software systems. He has designed a stand-alone MOOC platform called eLDa as part of the tool for his Doctorate research investigation. Dr Onah has produced over 18 publications to date and currently working on some publications in-progress on his research. His research has been recognised nationally and internationally by researchers, academics, professionals and have been cited in several international journals and conference publications. Dr Onah has chaired sessions at several international conferences, blind-peer- reviewed academic papers (Journals & Conferences) and is a member of programme committees for many conferences. Dr Onah in June 2017 visited King’s College London, Instructional Design and Development, King’s Online department as a visiting researcher. During Dr Onah’s visit, he initiated a collaborative opportunity with the team on adaptive systems and other related research interests. Dr Onah at the end of June 2017 visited University College London, Department of Computer Science as a visiting researcher. The discussion during Dr Onah’s visit was on his recent research on eLDa MOOC and also on a collaborative project on the evaluation of learners acceptability of an existing online system done in partnership with other European partner institutions. We discussed potential project and a specific area of research collaboration.
